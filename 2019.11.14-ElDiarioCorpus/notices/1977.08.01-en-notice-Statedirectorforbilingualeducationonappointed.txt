State director for bilingual education on appointed.The Colorado Board of Education Tuesday appointed Mrs. Rosalie Martinez of Fort Lupton as the state director of bilingual education. Mrs. Martinez heads the bilingual program for Fort Lupton area schools, she has held the position for the past five years. Mrs. Martinez has been a teacher in the area since 1957.The state bi-lingual program administers more than $2 million in state funds to local programs and also provides technical assistance.. Three top officials of the program resigned April 11, saying their philosophy of bilingual education differed from that of the state Department of Education.
