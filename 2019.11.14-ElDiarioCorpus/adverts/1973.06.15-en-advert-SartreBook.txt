A brief, incisive sketch of Sartre's life and personality precedes a brilliant analysis of the development of this many thought, beginning in 1940. Hazel Barnes examines each of his principal literary works, tracing the evolution of his philosophy and unity of thought, and relating his early existentialism to his later Marxism. The author, Hazel E. Barnes, Is a professor of Classics at the University of Colorado and one of the leading experts on Sartre In the English-speaking world. She has written two major works on existentialism; An Existentialist Ethics and Humanistic Existentialism. She has also authored The University as the New Church. SARTRE by Hazel E. Barnes J. B.
